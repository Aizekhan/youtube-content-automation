🎣 HOOK GENERATION (Scene 1 ONLY)

The FIRST SCENE must include a powerful HOOK that grabs attention in 10-15 seconds.

Hook Requirements:
- 1-3 sentences maximum (50-120 characters)
- Creates immediate curiosity, shock, mystery, or intrigue
- NO context or explanation (pure mystery)
- Dramatic pacing with pause after
- Strong sensory or emotional language

Hook Types by Genre:

HORROR:

MYSTERY / CRIME:

MYTHOLOGY / FANTASY:

SCI-FI:

PHILOSOPHY / REFLECTION:

DOCUMENTARY / HISTORY:

🎙️ SSML GENERATION RULES (AWS Polly Neural)

For each scene paragraph_text, generate ssml_text with AWS Polly SSML markup.

Available SSML Tags:

1. PAUSES: <break time="Xms"/> or <break time="Xs"/>
   - After normal sentences: 300ms-800ms
   - After dramatic statements: 1s-2s
   - Scene transitions: 800ms-1.5s
   - End of scene: 1s-2s

2. WHISPER: <amazon:effect name="whispered">text</amazon:effect>
   Use for: mystery, secrets, fear, intimacy, supernatural
   Best for: horror, psychological, mystery genres

3. SPEED: <prosody rate="VALUE">text</prosody>
   Values: x-slow, slow, medium, fast, x-fast
   
   Match narrative_pace from config:
   - slow → use "slow" or "x-slow"
   - medium → use "medium"
   - fast → use "fast"

4. PITCH: <prosody pitch="VALUE">text</prosody>
   Values: -10% to +10%
   
   Guidelines:
   - Dark/horror: -10% to -5% (lower, ominous)
   - Neutral: -2% to +2%
   - Epic/bright: +5% to +10% (higher, hopeful)

5. VOLUME: <prosody volume="VALUE">text</prosody>
   Values: soft, medium, loud
   
   - soft: whispers, intimate, fear
   - medium: default
   - loud: dramatic reveals, emphasis

6. EMPHASIS: <emphasis level="VALUE">text</emphasis>
   Values: moderate, strong
   
   Use for key words, plot points, names
   Maximum 2-3 times per scene

7. NESTING: You can combine tags
   <prosody rate="slow" pitch="-5%">
     <amazon:effect name="whispered">
       Dark whispered text
     </amazon:effect>
   </prosody>

SSML Best Practices:
✓ Match tts_mood_tags from channel_config
✓ Respect narrative_pace (slow = more pauses, slower rate)
✓ Use whisper strategically (not every sentence)
✓ Vary prosody to avoid monotony
✓ Always wrap in <speak></speak> tags
✓ Add pauses for dramatic effect
✓ Don't overuse emphasis

SSML Example (Horror - Slow Pace):
<speak>
<prosody rate="slow" pitch="-3%">I bought the mirror at an estate sale three weeks ago.</prosody>
<break time="600ms"/>
The seller, an elderly woman with trembling hands, told me it had belonged to her grandmother.
<break time="800ms"/>
<prosody pitch="-5%">She didn't meet my eyes when she said it.</prosody>
<break time="1s"/>
The mirror was beautiful—<emphasis level="moderate">ornate gold frame, slightly tarnished</emphasis>, with delicate roses carved into the corners.
<break time="600ms"/>
When I asked why she was selling it,
<break time="400ms"/>
<amazon:effect name="whispered"><prosody rate="x-slow">she only said, 'It doesn't belong in my house anymore.'</prosody></amazon:effect>
<break time="1.5s"/>
<emphasis level="strong">I should have listened.</emphasis>
<break time="1s"/>
</speak>
